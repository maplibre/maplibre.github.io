> **TL;DR:**
> **MapLibre** is **re-envisioning the bounty program** as originally conceived because it isn't a sustainable way to incentivize contributions at our current scale.
> We're replacing it with a **contributor recognition system**.
> We will honor any existing bounty commitments.

Since our founding, MapLibre has asked sponsors to contribute to a development fund intended to incentivize new feature development; primarily through bounties. After running this program for several years, we've concluded it no longer serves our community well.

The Governing Board's mission is to sustain and grow this project by recognizing those who invest their time and money in it. A bounty-based model may have seemed like a good idea at first, but at our current scale it creates more problems than it solves.

We are replacing the bounty program with a contributor recognition system that rewards the regular, high-value contributions that sustain and strengthen our community. We believe this is the right move for MapLibre and its community, and it reflects the model most sustainable open source projects have adopted. If you have questions, please [reach out](mailto:board@maplibre.org).

## Our future direction

Effective March 31, 2026, we will replace the bounty system with quarterly contributor recognition, including modest financial prizes.

A few things to set expectations:

- **Judgment is qualitative, not metric-based.** The Board and maintainers will evaluate impact and community value; not pull request counts, lines of code, or similar proxies.
- **Salaried contributors** are eligible for recognition, but not cash prizes.
- **Prize amounts** will be comparable to historical bounty payouts and balanced against responsible budgeting priorities.
- **Award determinations** will be made by the Board, with input from maintainers, and will be final.

This program will evolve as we learn what works and as our funding situation changes.

## Reason for the change

We are making this change because the system as it was designed fails on a structural level:

- **Overhead:** The bureaucratic overhead is high for both MapLibre and our community.
- **Visibility:** Many very helpful contributions happen outside this system and remain invisible to it, such as community work and ecosystem maintenance.
- **AI:** We need to respond to the rising use of coding agents and AI assisted development; the Bounty System does not adequately incentivize the patterns we need from contributors.
- **Compensation:** The payouts are not competitive enough to be a meaningful or reliable income source in most markets.
- **Adoption:** We have not seen significant usage of this system for contributions.

## For ecosystem maintainers

Ecosystem integrations (Flutter, Qt, Compose, React Native, and others) extend MapLibre into developer communities our core team could not reach on our own. Without the community maintainers who keep these integrations working, those ecosystems would go unserved.

Ecosystem maintainers are eligible for quarterly recognition and prizes alongside core contributors. We also support you in the following ways:

- **Exclusive merch** for active integration maintainers.
- **Hardware and testing resources**: If you're blocked by a missing device or platform, reach out and we'll help where we can.
- **Conference travel**: We can cover reasonable costs for presenting your work (contact a [board member](/about) first).
- **Grants**: We're exploring grant-based funding; if you're interested in submitting a proposal, a [board member](/about) will help you write it.

We'd pay you if we could, and we're working toward it.

## Looking ahead

MapLibre exists because of its contributors across code, ecosystem integrations, and community work. That hasn't changed.

This shift is about being honest about what we can sustain today, while focusing our resources where they have the most impact.

Our goal is a well-funded project that can pay the people who sustain it. That starts with sponsorship; if your company builds on MapLibre, please [become a sponsor](/sponsors).

MapLibre remains a community-driven project. If you have feedback or ideas, we want to hear from you. This is an ongoing conversation, and we're building the future together.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What is the history of the bounty program?**

MapLibre introduced our bounty program in 2022 to give back to contributors, encourage new participation, and create a lightweight reward system. Developers could sign up, comment on bounty-labeled issues, complete the work, and submit an invoice through OpenCollective. The process was bureaucratic by design, and at the time, our funds were limited and we needed to manage commitments carefully. We are more established today, and want to align with the common practices of other open source organizations.

**Q: Don't contributors deserve to know in advance whether they'll be compensated?**

Yes, and that is exactly why we're making this change. The bounty system implied a level of financial predictability we could not deliver. The new system is honest about what we can sustain: recognition, visibility, and prizes calibrated to actual impact, awarded on a quarterly basis.

**Q: Is this part of a broader organizational change?**

No. MapLibre remains a lean organization with a Governing Board made entirely of volunteers and four part-time staff members.

Our financial reports are public. We are currently operating at a loss. See our [sponsor page](/sponsors) if you want to help.

**Q: What is "high quality"?**

High-quality contributions augment the capabilities of our core SDKs, provide utility to significant numbers of users and developers downstream, and do so without sacrificing performance or adding unnecessary complexity. We also value contributions that keep community engagement high and operational overhead low, and these are often harder to measure but have an outsized long-term impact.

A high-quality contribution is one where a maintainer can look at the result and say, "This was good work."

Examples:

- [@wayofthefuture's](https://github.com/wayofthefuture) work on [GeoJSON performance in `maplibre-gl-js`](https://github.com/maplibre/maplibre-gl-js/pulls?q=is%3Apr+author%3Awayofthefuture+geojson)
- [@NathanMOlson's](https://github.com/NathanMOlson) work to [enable camera roll in `maplibre-native`](https://github.com/maplibre/maplibre-native/pull/3011)
- [@alasram's](https://github.com/alasram) work to [implement LOD controls in `maplibre-native`](https://github.com/maplibre/maplibre-native/pull/2958)

**Q: What is an "active contributor"?**

"Active contributor" is intentionally loosely defined. This makes the system non-gameable and allows us to recognize the wide variety of work our community does. "Active" does not mean frequent. A single, large, high-impact contribution makes you an active part of MapLibre's success.

**Q: How is an "active contributor" different from a voting member?**

Voting members elect the Governing Board and vote on changes to the MapLibre Charter. Every active contributor is made a [voting member](https://github.com/maplibre/maplibre/blob/main/CHARTER.md#voting-members), but voting members are not automatically active contributors.

**Q: Will non-code contributors be recognized?**

Yes. Non-code contributions are often more valuable than code contributions and are frequently overlooked. A well-written issue or documentation improvement can have an outsized effect on community growth. If you see someone being overlooked, reach out to a [board member](/about).

**Q: Why not use an objective threshold, "if this, then that"?**

We want to avoid the Hacktoberfest problem: metric-chasing that creates the wrong incentives and burdens maintainers with low-effort contributions or subtle regressions. Qualitative judgment keeps the bar meaningful.

**Q: What if I have an in-flight bounty?**

Contact your maintainer. We will honor existing commitments.
